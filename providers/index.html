Symptoms in. Classification out.

Patients don’t need to know what counts as a migraine. Ember asks about symptoms, impact, and medication; the ICHD‑3 classification is automatic, and quietly explained over time, so they start to recognize it themselves.

Migraine day

No AI inside. All logic is hard-coded to published guidelines and studies — fully auditable. See references.

Each treatment, alone and combined.

When patients are on overlapping preventives, Ember tracks each medication’s contribution and the combined effect. Botox alone might be a 40% reduction. A CGRP alone, 40%. Together — 78%.

Ember reports the numbers cleanly, so the case for continuing therapy is easier to make. The continuation decisions are yours.

Overuse, watched gently.

Ember tracks acute medication use against overuse thresholds and offers nonjudgmental nudges as patients approach the limits. The nudges are easy to turn off.

Reports what they logged. Nothing more.

Ember structures data; it doesn’t interpret it. The patient’s logs stay on their phone — we never see them. The clinical decisions remain yours.
